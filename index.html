I got my bachelor degree from Tongji University, Shanghai, China. I also visited University of Alberta in Canada funded by CSC and Canadian government program during undergraduate. Currently, I'm a visiting researcher at Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute, affiliated with Department of Computing Science of University of Alberta .

I work closely with researchers from several institutes, including Prof. Randy Goebel from University of Alberta , Prof. Tianwei Yu from Emory University, Prof. Min Yang from Chinese Academy of Sciences, Prof. Ye Luo from Tongji University and Dr. Nicholas Jing Yuan from Microsoft.

My primary research interest lies at the intersection of Deep Learning and Natural Language Processing (NLP). Recently, I've been working on abstractive text summarization and rule-based extraction from Wikipedia. I'm also interested and had experience in Bioinformatics and Social Network Analysis.
